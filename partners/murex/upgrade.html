FAQ

Murex (MX.3) is a leading capital markets solution for sell-side organizations. With more than 60,000 daily users globally, including at 65 of the world’s top 100 banks, Murex is a cross-asset platform that supports 300+ ready-to-use payoffs.

Murex’s asset coverage includes:

  • FX assets and FX options
  • Digital assets
  • Securities finance
  • Fixed income
  • Linear and non-linear rates
  • Inflation and credit
  • Funds and ETF
  • Alternative investments

Besides extensive asset coverage, Murex offers the following features to its users:

  • Powerful APIs for processing custom payoffs
  • Cross-functional integration from front-office to back-office
  • Modular architecture for tailoring the platform to the organization’s unique needs
  • Regulatory compliance out-of-the-box across the clearing, collateral, reporting, market, counterpart credit and liquidity risk requirements
  • Comprehensive IT strategy support, from IaaS and PaaS to BPaaS and SaaS models
  • Full and hybrid cloud readiness out-of-the-box

A Murex version upgrade allows you to reap the following benefits:

  • Up-to-date vendor support. If your current Murex version is no longer supported, it becomes a source of security vulnerabilities
  • Access to new features. Murex adds hundreds of new features to each quarterly MX.3 update that allow you to comply with the latest regulations
  • Improved efficiency. New Murex versions come with better performance, more robust automation capabilities, and user experience improvements to give your operational efficiency a boost
  • Enhanced customer experience. New functionality, along with improved efficiency, allows you to serve your customers more effectively
  • Future-proofed solution. Transitioning from a legacy solution to a modern Murex architecture allows you to further secure your investment into Murex and make the most out of it

Implementing automation and CI/CD for a Murex upgrade:

  • Speeds up the upgrade process. Version control and release automation accelerate the integration and deployment, all while mitigating risks and ensuring software quality
  • Reduces costs. While implementing automation and CI/CD is an investment in and of itself, you’ll recoup the costs as the upgrade will require fewer resources and be completed faster
  • Ensures quality. Automation paves the way for standardization, which, in turn, reduces the risk of human error and maximizes quality assurance efficiency

This step typically involves a single-time investment into new automation tools and revamping some workflows or whole processes. You can implement automation and CI/CD for a Murex upgrade in multiple ways, including but not limited to the vendor’s MxCI toolkit.
