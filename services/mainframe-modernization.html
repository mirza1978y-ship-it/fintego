Our mainframe modernization services

 

Work with fintego to modernize your mainframe — cut costs, reduce risk and ease your mind, while preserving your past investments and years of accumulated business logic. With advanced open-source technologies, comprehensive test coverage and continuous integration, our expert engineers will help future-proof your organization by modernizing your applications on the mainframe or moving them off the mainframe. 

 

fintego’s modernization process

Assessment

fintego’s experts will assess your legacy estate and identify the most suitable mainframe legacy modernization path for your system. Our tried and tested methodology can be adapted and applied to any mainframe modernization project, regardless of the technologies involved. 

Estimation

We’ll create a modernization strategy and help you understand the scope, timeline and effort involved. Our engineering teams will work with IT and business stakeholders to set and maintain realistic expectations for the work ahead. 

Code conversion

Switch to a modern tech stack with fintego’s automated code conversion services. With a combination of human expertise and AI-powered automation, we’ll help you speed up your mainframe modernization and integration. Our toolkits cover multiple technologies, including COBOL, JCL and assembler code conversion. We can also convert CA Gen–based systems.  

Migration

Retain your mainframe investments and business logic by porting your legacy mainframe applications. We’ll ensure your applications work smoothly in a new environment, open-source or proprietary, via an environment emulation solution.

Modernizing a mission-critical COBOL/IMS mainframe application 

 

A Fortune 500 client was implementing a data center transition project when one mainframe application jeopardized it. Although several attempts to modernize the application were taken, ​none resulted​​​ in a successful transition.  

The client turned to fintego with a request to assist them and another vendor in quality assurance on a consulting basis. Eventually, we took over the migration and:  

  • Switched the operating system from zOS to RHEL  
  • Auto-transformed JCL to Python  
  • Implemented an MSDB-to-SQL adapter  
  • Optimized application performance with GnuCOBOL compiler optimization, caching, and database and business logic optimization  
  • Implemented a robust CI/CD pipeline

FAQ

Mainframe modernization is the process of improving the maintainability, integration capabilities and cost-efficiency of mainframe applications by adapting them to a more modern tech stack. Modernizing your mainframe allows you to reap the following six benefits: 

  • Reduced operational costs 
  • Accelerated time-to-market for application changes 
  • Improved maintainability and facilitated support 
  • Facilitated data flows and integrations 
  • Improved business agility and resilience 
  • Enhanced security and streamlined compliance 

Common approaches toward mainframe application modernization are called the seven Rs: 

  • Retiring applications 
  • Retaining the mainframe and modernizing peripherals 
  • Replacing mainframe applications with a commercial off-the-shelf platform 
  • Rewriting applications from scratch using a modern tech stack 
  • Rehosting, or lifting and shifting applications to a new environment 
  • Rearchitecting your mainframe by reusing business logic in a new application written using modern technologies 
  • Reengineering your mainframe by combining multiple approaches listed above 

A variety of mainframe applications can be optimized during mainframe modernization, such as: 

  • Customer order processing applications 
  • Financial transaction processing systems 
  • Production and inventory control systems 
  • Payroll management applications 
  • Medical patient record systems with personally identifiable information (PII) 
  • Government record systems 

Mainframes are mainly used by financial institutions, healthcare organizations, government agencies and, to some extent, retailers. Hence, organizations in these sectors stand to benefit the most from mainframe modernization in terms of data security, performance, resilience and agility. 

The scope of a mainframe modernization project can vary significantly based on: 

  • Application complexity and the current tech stack 
  • Chosen application modernization approach (rehost/rewrite/rearchitect/etc.) 
  • Automation tools and accelerators involved in the process 

The project scope will, in turn, determine how much time modernization requires — and that duration will differ from one organization to another. Therefore, if you want to estimate how long your mainframe modernization should take, it’s best to discuss your needs with a modernization partner and request an estimate. 

Contact our modernization experts to talk about your current and desired mainframe states, and we’ll get back to you with a high-level project timeline. 

Any mainframe modernization undertaking has to address the following three risks: 

  • Exceeded budget and/or missed deadlines. This is usually the result of an unclear or unrealistic modernization scope. Therefore, mitigating this risk requires a proper assessment of the modernization efforts required to reach the desired state, as well as proper coordination between the C-suite and IT leadership. 
  • Failure to meet expectations. When the chosen approach isn’t the most suitable one, modernization results can fail to attain the set goals. To avoid this scenario, the current and desired states have to be carefully assessed to identify the most optimal modernization approach. 
  • Functionality deterioration. This can come in the form of broken dependencies, errors and bugs, or business logic and data lost in the modernization process. Addressing this risk means paying extra attention to dependencies, interoperability and data flows.
