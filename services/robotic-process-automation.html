That said, integrating RPA tools with existing systems comes with several challenges. Implementing screen scraping techniques may require oversight and fine-tuning to ensure accuracy. Adding an RPA tool to the existing infrastructure may also expose sensitive data, so data security and compliance require extra attention. On top of all of that, you’ll need to ensure your solution’s scalability and maintainability in the long run. 

To discuss the feasibility and specifics of integrating RPA tools with your particular systems, don’t hesitate to get in touch with us. Our subject matter experts will get back to you to identify the most suitable path for leveraging RPA within your IT estate.

If you implement a robotic process automation as-a-service solution, the vendor takes on most of the technical support and maintenance, including performance optimization, security, and compliance updates. On your end, you may need to take care of system configurations, monitor system performance, manage incidents, and maintain integrations with existing systems. 

If your RPA solution is bespoke, in addition to your responsibilities under the COTS solution, you’ll also have to take on technical updates and feature enhancements. These updates have to maintain the solution’s performance, compliance, security and scalability.
