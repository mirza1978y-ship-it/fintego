Cumbersome test maintenance

Teams struggle to balance keeping automation suites up to date while continuing to automate tests for new functionality at an even pace. In addition, teams must address overheads like test bloat (redundant tests), dealing with false positives and negatives in the test results, and performing root-cause analysis.


Test automation and high-quality QA reduce manual tasks preventing teams from focusing on what matters most — innovation.

Unstable test environments

Enterprise ecosystems consist of multiple downstream and upstream systems that are in a state of continuous development and release. That makes it difficult to create and follow a sustainable, end-to-end test approach until version control and the DevOps process have achieved maturity.


An automation testing company company with solid expertise in test automation can help teams resolve these complex, high maintenance environments to improve the development cycle.

Out-of-date test data

To create an effective test automation suite that addresses all requirements, companies must maintain and refresh test data in an efficient way, combining the creation of synthetic test data with the use of production-like data to test.


Resolving the underlying test data challenge drives the scalability and data integrity testing required of cloud migration and digital transformation. fintego offers high-quality QA automation services to help you overcome this challenge.

FAQ

Quality assurance plays a crucial role at all stages of software development, including: 

  • Ensuring all technical requirements are testable 
  • Detecting unpredictable behavior of the application early on and signaling it to the development team 
  • Preventing errors, bugs​,​ and failures from making it into the final product 
  • Ensuring the application is user-friendly and provides an excellent user experience 
  • Preventing compatibility issues in case of using multiple technologies or updating the software 
  • Ensuring data flows properly between multiple components of the application 
  • Identifying and signaling any potential exploits or security issues before deployment 

Shift-left testing is an approach to software development where test engineers get involved in the project early on. They review the technical requirements and ensure their testability, thus minimizing the risk of quality issues remaining unidentified later in development. 

This early-on collaboration also allows for a faster feedback loop between issue detection and its solution. As a result, shift-left testing speeds up the development process, makes it more agile and scalable, and enables continuous testing. 

Our quality engineering framework is built on five pillars: 

  • Continuous improvement. We don’t rest on our laurels. Instead, we keep perfecting our tools, processes, and products to ensure our services always provide maximum value. 
  • Client-centric approach. No two organizations are the same, and neither are our testing solutions. We involve domain experts to ensure our solutions match our client’s needs perfectly. 
  • Data-driven decision-making. We set up an automated system that gives our clients valuable insights into product quality and enables them to make well-informed decisions. 
  • End-to-end testing. We cover every aspect of the system with our software test automation services to ensure its full reliability and quality. 
  • Risk-based prioritization. We maximize testing ROI and efficiency by prioritizing requirements and focusing on testing critical components first.

  • Dedicate their resources to core activities while the vendor takes on quality assurance

  • Gain a fresh perspective on your codebase and testing practices
