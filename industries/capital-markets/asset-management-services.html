FAQ

Asset management services can help your organization: 

  • Reduce technology estate complexity by removing functional duplication and retiring or substituting applications 
  • Gain a 360-degree of every customer by breaking data silos to enhance CX and derive customer insights with advanced analytics 
  • Improve client lifecycle management with industry-leading CLM and KYC solutions 
  • Deliver superior, hyper-personalized, omnichannel customer experiences 
  • Ramp up data monitoring and visualization tools to improve client reporting, performance attribution, capital use, etc. 
  • Streamline regulatory compliance across jurisdictions and lines of business with intelligent automation 
  • Ensure business continuity and resilience with futureproof systems and automated testing/QA 
  • Modernize technology estate by reducing application complexity and replacing legacy systems with bespoke solutions or off-the-shelf platforms 
  • Enable data-driven decision-making with advanced analytics and reporting capabilities 
  • Automate processes to improve employee experience and productivity across the organization, from customer service agents to financial advisors 

Whether meant for the asset manager team, financial advisors or compliance managers, asset management solutions can help reduce costs by: 

  • Automating manual processes from front- to back-office, increasing efficiency and employee productivity, and reducing operational costs 
  • Ensuring a seamless flow of data between systems and, therefore, teams, improving productivity as well 
  • Streamlining workflows by simplifying certain processes and improving user experience for employees 
  • Maximizing resource allocation efficiency with real-time advanced data analytics for the use of capital 
  • Identifying and removing operational inefficiencies with process mining and AI-powered analytics 
  • Ensuring the most optimal match between customer demand and the organization’s offerings 
  • Reducing technology estate complexity, bringing down the TCO and improving ROI as a result 

Consider these six factors when looking for your future asset management service vendor: 

  • Product knowledge: Analyze the vendor’s experience with the type of solution you’re envisioning and its underlying technologies (e.g., data analytics for portfolio management) 
  • Service range: Compare the company’s range of services against your needs and ensure a match (implementation, integration, managed services, bespoke engineering, etc.) 
  • Partner network: Consider the company’s partnerships with leading tools, platforms, and service providers 
  • Industry experience: Assess the company’s experience in your industry and niche to ensure it has a good grasp of business processes, compliance requirements, and operational constraints 
  • Client scope: Look into the vendor’s experience with similar clients (e.g., financial advisors, sovereign wealth funds, pension funds) and consider the projects’ scale and scope 
  • Portfolio: Browse the vendor’s case studies to gain a better grasp of their experience in your domain (e.g., financial advisor solutions)
