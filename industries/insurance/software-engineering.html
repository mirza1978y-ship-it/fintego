In our experience, there are eight distinct prerequisites of a successful collaboration with a service vendor:

  • Contracts and commercials are verified for alignment with business goals, as determined by key stakeholders
  • Talent generation is enabled when the team possesses a well-rounded skill set to handle the scope of work and deliver the desired results
  • Knowledge transition between the vendor and client teams is a part of the process
  • The engagement operating model is founded on the clear distribution of tasks, roles and responsibilities between the parties
  • Scope and demand management is based on a well-defined framework and the objectives and engagement model are aligned with the client’s needs
  • Engineering performance is continuously tracked using key performance indicators aligned with objectives
  • Governance is jointly drafted up, unified and aligned with the collaboration model
  • Transformation occurs when the vendor strives to add value to the client well beyond the contractual commitments

Here are the four ways productive and efficient software development can boost insurers’ operational efficiency and help them reduce costs:

  • Improved flexibility and adaptability. Efficient software development means a faster time-to-market. So, new features and products that aim to improve operational efficiency can be rolled out promptly and at scale
  • Mitigated risks. Agile and DevOps best practices ensure that all risks are considered before the coding begins. This helps avoid costly mistakes or false assumptions
  • Streamlined processes. Automated testing and development reduce the needed development time and headcount needs, reducing associated costs. They also ensure stakeholders see results fast
  • Facilitated compliance. With faster time-to-market for software updates, insurers can adapt their systems to new regulatory requirements promptly, avoiding hefty fines
